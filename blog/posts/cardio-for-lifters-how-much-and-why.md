A lot of lifters avoid cardio because they think it kills gains. In practice, moderate cardio can improve work capacity, heart health, and recovery between sets.

You do not need marathon training. Two to three low to moderate sessions per week is enough for most people. Think brisk walking, cycling, incline treadmill, or easy rowing.

Keep high-intensity cardio limited if strength is your main priority. It is effective, but also fatiguing. Place it away from heavy lower-body sessions when possible.

Cardio can also help you recover by improving circulation and your ability to handle training volume. Better conditioning often means better gym sessions.

If your schedule is packed, even short sessions count. Ten to twenty minutes done consistently adds up quickly.

Strength and cardio are not enemies. They can complement each other really well when planned intelligently.
