Rest times are not just a random pause between sets. They decide how much quality output you can produce in the next set.

If your goal is strength on heavy compounds, longer rests usually win. Two to five minutes can be completely reasonable for squats, presses, and deadlifts. You need enough recovery to perform well.

For hypertrophy work, you can often use shorter rests, around 60 to 120 seconds, especially on accessories. This keeps sessions moving while still allowing hard sets.

Too little rest can look hardcore but hurt results. If your performance crashes set to set, your effective volume drops. You might feel tired, but tired is not automatically productive.

A practical strategy: longer rest for big lifts, shorter rest for isolation work. Use a timer so your rest stays intentional, not random.

You do not get extra points for rushing. Rest enough to do good reps with good intent.
