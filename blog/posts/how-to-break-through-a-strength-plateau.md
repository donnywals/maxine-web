Hitting a plateau is normal. It does not mean you are done progressing. It usually means your current setup stopped creating enough adaptation.

First, check recovery basics. Are you sleeping enough? Eating enough protein and calories? Managing stress? If recovery is poor, programming tweaks will only do so much.

Next, review training variables. You might need more volume, less volume, or a temporary intensity shift. Small changes beat total program rewrites.

Exercise variation can help too. If your bench is stuck, add close-grip bench or paused bench for a block. If squats stall, front squats or tempo squats can address weak positions.

Deloads also matter. Sometimes your strength is hidden by accumulated fatigue. A lighter week can restore performance quickly.

Most importantly, give changes enough time to work. Evaluate over weeks, not single sessions. Plateaus are puzzles, not dead ends.
