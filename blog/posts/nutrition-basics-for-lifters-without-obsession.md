Nutrition for lifting does not need to be complicated. Most people get great results from doing a few things consistently.

Start with protein. A practical target is around 1.6 to 2.2 grams per kilogram of body weight per day. Spread it across meals so recovery support is steady.

Then handle calories based on your goal. Want to gain muscle faster? Small surplus. Want to lose fat while lifting? Small deficit and keep protein high. Extreme cuts and bulks are usually unnecessary.

Carbs are useful for training performance. If workouts feel flat, under-eating carbs is often the reason. Fats matter too for hormones and general health.

Do not ignore hydration and basic food quality. You can hit macros and still feel terrible if sleep, hydration, and micronutrients are neglected.

Aim for consistency, not perfection. Good nutrition repeated for months beats strict dieting that collapses after two weeks.
