Soreness is not a progress metric. It is just a response to unfamiliar or challenging training. You can be very sore and make little progress. You can be barely sore and make great progress.

Early in a program, soreness is more common. Your body adapts quickly, and soreness usually drops while performance improves. That is normal and good.

Instead of chasing soreness, track useful signals: load, reps, set quality, recovery between sessions, and long-term trends. Those actually tell you if training works.

Recovery basics still matter a lot. Sleep, nutrition, hydration, and smart programming determine whether hard training turns into adaptation.

If soreness is extreme and lingering, it is often a sign volume or novelty is too high. Adjust down slightly and rebuild.

Progress is performance over time, not how wrecked you feel the day after leg day.
