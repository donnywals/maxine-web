Why Maxine?

Measuring increases the likelihood of reaching your goals

Tracking progress is the fastest way to hit your fitness goals, whether you’re chasing a heavier squat or a higher one rep max. Writing targets down, reviewing them often, and seeing clear trends keeps you accountable and focused during every workout.

With Maxine, you set precise goals for each lift, see performance against your personal best, and get guidance to close the gap. Every session delivers the workout goal tracker insights you need—no guesswork, just actionable data that keeps you moving toward the next milestone.

One-rep max insights

Maxine’s one-rep max tracker and calculator show what you can safely lift today. Watching that number rise turns progress into something you can trust and removes guesswork about what to attempt next.

By surfacing personal best trends and recent performance, you get a feedback loop that encourages smart effort—not reckless jumps. Each estimate acts as a checkpoint: push when you’re ready, dial back when you’re fatigued, and steadily stack small wins that compound into a stronger one rep max.

Frictionless by design

We keep logging, summaries, and defaults effortless so you stay focused on your workout, not the app. Quick-entry flows, sensible presets, and automatic aggregation mean fewer taps and less context switching mid-session. You stay in the zone, with insights ready the moment you need them.

By stripping away unnecessary steps, Maxine reduces mental load and lets you concentrate on form, effort, and recovery. The result: less time fiddling with your phone, more time lifting, and cleaner fitness goals data that’s ready to turn into actionable guidance every time you train.

Frequently asked questions

Is my data secure?
Yes. Maxine does not log or track any of your data. This means that all your workout information is securely stored on your device.
Does Maxine use subscriptions?
No. Maxine is a one-time purchase app with no subscriptions or in-app purchases.
How does Maxine create insights?
Maxine uses a mix of AI and simle, local, code to analyze your exercises. AI is used to provide summaries. To generate insights, there's local code that will check things like how often you update your weight data, and how far off you are from your goals. Insights are merely suggestions that you can choose to follow. Note that you should always take care of your body when working out instead of always following through on every insight immediately. Maxine does not provide medical advice.
